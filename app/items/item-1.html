Portraits

Some of the richest and most vivid paintings of wonder cabinets, or kunstkammer, are those by Frans Francken the Younger (1581-1642). In capturing the sumptuous nature of these cabinets, Francken also depicted the numerous portraits within them, thought to be the owners or collectors. Portraits, which historically depicted the wealthy and influential, were usually intended to flatter the sitter, and their construction was intensely careful and controlled.

In our modern digital wonder cabinets, we are in control of our own portraits – our profile pictures. We can change, emphasise or obscure certain features. Choosing the right profile picture can reveal so much about who we are, and what matters to us. People who have used social media sites over a prolonged period of time will have chosen multiple profile pictures, curating a gallery of images or series of performances.

With the rise of the smartphone a new connection between the portrait and the profile picture has emerged – the #museumselfie phenomenon. Museum selfies usually feature the visitor capturing the moment they interact with a famous portrait or painting. In this way, the digital wonder cabinet functions as a means of showing a person's good taste in seeking out other objects of meaning and value.
